2. Executive Summary
This version substantially deepens the earlier PRD after a discovery call with the business's operations/accounts manager, who currently runs the entire stock, labour, cost, and vendor-tracking process for the company largely from memory and WhatsApp groups.
The business is a building-materials supply and site-execution contractor operating roughly 11 sites in total — 4 under the manager's direct responsibility and 7 managed on behalf of other parties — plus oversight of 3 related production units (a plywood business, an umbrella/galvanized-sheet unit, and a chemical/adhesive unit) and 2 company vehicles, all currently coordinated by one person.
The call surfaced a much richer picture of the real cost and accountability chain than the Excel files alone showed: multi-vendor material sourcing, engineering-based billing reconciliation, multi-site labour cost allocation, a daily site cash/capital ledger, vehicle and compliance tracking, and a chronic single-person bottleneck. This PRD updates scope, workflows, and data model to reflect that reality, while keeping the system focused on operational tracking — formal financial/GST accounting continues to sit in the company's existing Tally system, outside this application's scope.
3. Background — Current State (Updated)
3.1 From the Excel file review (recap)
Each site has its own workbook; no shared structure, no running stock balance, inconsistent units.
Tools/equipment tracked only at some sites by hour-meter/trip readings for costing.
Labour attendance is a daily name list; wages/advances tracked in separate per-contractor sheets.
3.2 From the manager's discovery call — the real day-to-day process
Before a project starts: travel expenses to visit/survey the site, and a local market survey of nearby hardware/building-material shops and their rates — this record is sent back and used once the tender is approved.
Once confirmed, a purchase list is drawn up: sand, stone, cement, rod/TMT (with size variations like 10mm/8mm/12mm), shuttering plywood and battens, binding wire, nails, paint, and general hardware.
Before material even starts moving, a labour camp is set up at the site: worker accommodation or a rented room, an office structure (bamboo/tin or rented), electrical setup, and camp supplies (cooking utensils, gas, bedding) plus worker PPE (jacket, helmet, shoes, gloves).
Ad-hoc site needs come up during execution and get purchased reactively (e.g., 50 raincoats/ponchos bought for workers during unseasonal rain at one site, better-quality raincoats for supervisors).
Daily grocery/food expense at each site is updated continuously.
A single material requirement is often fulfilled by multiple vendors simultaneously (e.g., 3 sand suppliers), because one supplier alone cannot meet the full quantity — each vendor's contribution and running payment balance must be tracked separately, in addition to a combined "mother figure" total received at the site.
Many small/unorganised suppliers (common outside West Bengal, described as the local "syndicate" system) do not issue a proper invoice at the time of delivery — only a handwritten khata note agreed between supplier and site owner. The manager tracks these running balances informally (in Excel) until a formal, proper invoice is eventually available; only transactions with a proper invoice are entered into the company's formal Tally books. This PRD treats that informal running balance as a legitimate accounts-payable tracking need — final financial/GST reporting remains a matter for the company's accountant and Tally system, outside this application's scope.
Stock-out of material must be tagged with purpose and exact location of use (e.g., wall, footing/foundation, beam, shuttering, above/below existing-ground-level) so it can later be reconciled against the client billing quantity, which is calculated from engineering "abstract" sheets (quantities derived from drawings).
When actual material used doesn't match the billed/engineering quantity, the gap is first attributed to normal wastage; anything beyond that is flagged as likely misuse or loss in transit — a sensitive issue that is hard to prove and is generally not raised directly with site staff.
Labour attendance must record not just headcount but which contractor's crew, and — critically — where each worker actually worked that day, since one contractor's crew is often split across multiple sites/office in a single day. Cost must be apportioned to each site's budget based on this split, not simply assigned in full to whichever site recorded the attendance.
Each site sends a daily handwritten expense ledger (photographed and posted to a site WhatsApp group); a fixed daily "capital" amount is issued to each site supervisor, from which day-to-day expenses are paid and later itemised.
Money transfers from the office to a site must be recorded carefully to avoid double counting: the amount sent should be entered as "capital received" at the site, and expenses paid out of that capital recorded separately — not re-entered as if it were new spend on top of the capital already logged.
Costs are aggregated monthly per site across categories — material, labour, camp/grocery, travel, hardware, miscellaneous/consumables — and compared against what was billed to the client, to check whether the site is actually profitable after accounting for staff salaries.
Beyond the sites: company vehicle fuel and running costs are logged per vehicle, servicing schedules tracked via daily odometer readings from drivers, and truck/lorry tax and pollution-certificate expiry dates need monitoring, alongside company-level compliance dates (GST valuation renewal, Udyog/MSME registration renewal).
An office store room holds machinery/equipment and other assets; items issued out need to be tracked as returnable or not, since equipment has previously gone missing without anyone accountable.
Structural/shed materials (a separate, larger category) include aluminium sheets, colour sheets, galvalume sheets, various beams (ISMB, ISMC), angles and channels in multiple sizes, foundation bolts, ridges, and more — often billed by square metre even though received by piece count, requiring unit-conversion calculations (piece → area → weight).
The entire process today is run by one person from memory and a phone group, with no per-site accountability — site-level staff (supervisors, engineers, labourers) generally do not track or even know their own site's current stock. The manager has proposed splitting this across a minimum of two to three office roles (accounts + machinery; site materials + billing) plus one accountable in-charge per site, so the load isn't concentrated in a single person.
3.3 From two further site registers (Baruipur, Salapuria Boundary Wall)
Every stock-in and transfer line already records a vehicle/car number (e.g., a specific registration number or a named vehicle like "Raju Tempo") — this is exactly the transport-accountability data point the manager described as a way to catch material going missing in transit, and should be a required field on transactions, not an afterthought.
Material moves directly site-to-site in routine practice (e.g., Salapuria to Green Ply, Salapuria to Austin), not only office-to-site — confirming transfers must support any site as both source and destination.
Hardware/consumable items (nails, screws, a toilet pan, a drill machine, AC repairing) are logged with a direct cost amount, effectively doubling as a running day-to-day expense ledger rather than pure stock-quantity tracking.
Tools/equipment returned to the office are explicitly logged, including items marked as broken/junk ("bekar machine") — confirming assets need a condition status (working/damaged), not just a location and returnable flag.
Labour records reveal a hierarchy deeper than "contractor → worker": a crew-lead ("Sarder") heads a sub-group under the contractor; entries split Mistry (skilled) vs Labour (general) headcounts, support half-day/fractional attendance (e.g., 8.5, 13 heads), capture task tags (e.g., "electric work"), and note advances inline against a specific day's attendance.
A miscellaneous "Other's" ledger captures large one-off vendor payments that don't fit any material category (e.g., a lump-sum payment to an iron trading company) — confirming the need for a true catch-all expense category.
4. Problem Statement (Updated)
All operational knowledge is concentrated in one manager; there is no shared, structured system, so training a replacement or adding staff currently takes months and rarely succeeds.
Sites are managed separately with no single place to view or manage them together, and site-level staff take no ownership of stock or costs at all.
Tools, materials, and vendor payments cannot be reliably tracked as they move between sites and vendors.
Material leftovers, wastage, and possible misuse are only discovered long after the fact (sometimes months later), when it's too late to act.
Labour cost cannot be accurately allocated per site when a contractor's crew works across multiple locations in a day.
Manual double-entry risk exists in the daily capital/expense ledger process.
The client wants a permanent, structured system that is still simple enough for site-level, non-technical staff to use daily.
5. Goals & Objectives
Provide one centralized system covering all sites (starting with the primary 8 discussed initially, extensible to the full ~11-site operation) accessible to Company Admin at any time.
Maintain a live, vendor-wise stock ledger per site per material, including multi-vendor fulfilment of a single requirement.
Enforce site-level accountability: whoever is on-site enters stock, labour, and expense data and owns it, rather than a central manager reconstructing everything from memory.
Track labour cost with per-site allocation, even when one contractor's crew is split across multiple sites.
Support billing reconciliation: compare material issued against engineering/billed quantities, and flag wastage or possible misuse for site-level review.
Track daily site capital and expenses with built-in double-counting prevention.
Provide monthly cost roll-ups per site (material + labour + camp + travel + hardware + misc) comparable against billing, to assess site-level profitability.
Support vehicle fleet and compliance-date tracking as a lightweight add-on module.
Keep the site-level data entry interface simple enough for supervisors and contractors with basic tech comfort.
6. Scope
6.1 In Scope (Phase 1)
Web application with role-based login for Company Admin, Site Supervisor, and Contractor.
Material master (with size/type variations), stock ledger, multi-vendor tracking, and inter-site stock transfer.
Tool/equipment master and location/usage tracking.
Labour master, daily attendance with multi-site allocation remarks, and contractor-wise crew and payment management.
Labour camp inventory and personal product/PPE distribution tracking.
Wastage/misuse flagging via stock-vs-billing reconciliation.
Purchase/vendor entry, including informal running balances for suppliers without a formal invoice yet.
Daily site capital and expense ledger, replacing the WhatsApp-photo process, with automatic capital-vs-expense separation to prevent double counting.
Monthly job/site cost roll-up and basic profitability view (cost vs. billed amount).
Transport log for material/labour movement between sites.
Dashboards and exportable reports, scoped by role.
6.2 In Scope (Phase 1, lightweight) — Fleet & Compliance
Vehicle master with fuel log, odometer readings, and servicing due dates.
Compliance calendar for vehicle tax/pollution certificate expiry and company-level renewal dates (GST valuation, Udyog registration), with reminders.
Office store/asset register with issued-to and returnable/non-returnable tracking.
6.3 Out of Scope (Phase 1)
Formal GST-compliant invoicing and financial accounting — remains in the existing Tally system; this application is not a replacement for statutory accounting.
The 3 side production units (plywood, umbrella/sheet, chemical) and the 2 personal-business vehicles — noted as a possible future module, pending client confirmation of whether they should be included.
Engineering BBS (Bar Bending Schedule) and full abstract-quantity calculation tools — Phase 1 will let users record billed vs. issued quantities for reconciliation, but won't perform engineering design calculations itself.
Biometric/RFID attendance hardware integration.
Offline-first mobile app (dependent on confirming site connectivity).
7. Business Process Overview (from Manager Discovery Call)
This section documents, in order, how a site's cost and material lifecycle actually flows today — this is the process the system needs to digitize.
7.1 Pre-Project
Travel to and survey the prospective site (main cost: travel/fuel/flight/train).
Survey local hardware/building-material shops for rates; record findings for later use.
On tender approval, retrieve the recorded local rates and finalize the purchase list.
7.2 Site Setup
Set up labour camp: accommodation, office structure, electrical, cooking/bedding supplies.
Procure worker PPE (helmets, jackets, shoes, gloves).
7.3 Material Procurement & Stock-In
Place orders for civil materials (sand, stone, cement, rod/TMT with size variants) and shuttering materials (ply, battens, binding wire, nails, paint, hardware).
Where one vendor can't fulfil full quantity, split across multiple vendors and track each vendor's delivered quantity and running payment balance separately, alongside a combined site-level received total.
Record vendor payments against each vendor's running balance as they're made.
7.4 Stock-Out, Usage & Reconciliation
Record material issued from site stock with purpose and exact location of use (e.g., wall, footing, beam, shuttering, above/below ground level).
At billing time, compare quantity issued against the client-billed/engineering abstract quantity for that structural element.
Any shortfall beyond expected wastage is flagged as possible misuse/loss for management review.
7.5 Labour & Contractor Cost
Record daily attendance by contractor/worker, with a remarks field capturing which site(s) each worker actually worked at that day.
Calculate wages due per contractor based on attendance, net of advances paid.
Apportion each contractor's total daily payment across the sites their crew actually worked at, rather than assigning it wholly to one site.
7.6 Daily Site Cash & Expense Ledger
Office issues a fixed daily capital amount to each site supervisor.
Capital received is recorded as "capital in" at the site — separately from expense entries — to prevent double counting.
Site expenses (grocery, labour payments, hardware, misc.) are logged daily against that capital, categorized (grocery, hardware, labour, travel, misc./consumables).
7.7 Monthly Roll-Up & Profitability
All cost categories (material, labour, camp, travel, hardware, misc.) are aggregated monthly per site.
Total site cost is compared against the amount billed to the client for that period to assess actual profitability after overheads.
8. User Roles & Personas
8.1 Company Admin
Full visibility and management rights across all sites, vendors, labour, and cost data. Uses the system mainly on desktop, for oversight, reconciliation, and reporting.
8.2 Project / Site Supervisor
Site-level user responsible for one (or a small number of) sites; the primary day-to-day data-entry user, needing the simplest possible mobile interface. Under the new design, the supervisor is expected to take real ownership of their site's stock and expense entries — closing the current gap where site staff don't track their own stock.
8.3 Contractor
Manages one or more labour crews. Marks/confirms attendance for their crew and records which site(s) each worker was actually deployed to on a given day, and views wages/advances for their crew.
9. Functional Requirements
9.1 Authentication & Access Control
Role-based login (Company Admin, Site Supervisor, Contractor); each Supervisor/Contractor account scoped to their assigned site(s)/crew only.
Audit log of who entered/edited what, and when.
9.2 Site & Material Master Data
Site master (site code, name, address, status) — extensible beyond the initial 8 sites.
Material master with category, standard unit, unit-conversion table (e.g., bag ↔ kg ↔ CFT), and support for size/type variations (e.g., 10mm/8mm/12mm rod; various beam, angle, and channel sizes).
9.3 Stock Management with Multi-Vendor Tracking
Live stock ledger per site per material: opening balance, in, out, current balance.
Multiple vendors can contribute to a single stock-in requirement; each vendor's delivered quantity, rate, and amount are tracked on a separate running ledger, in addition to a combined site total.
Vendor payments recorded against each vendor's ledger, showing running balance owed/paid — supporting vendors who haven't yet issued a formal invoice, tracked informally until a proper bill is available. Formal, invoice-backed transactions remain the responsibility of the company's existing Tally/accounting process.
Every stock-in and transfer transaction captures the delivering vehicle (registration number or named vehicle) — supporting transport-level accountability for material that goes missing in transit.
Hardware and consumable items can optionally carry a direct cost/amount per line, so day-to-day hardware purchases double as a lightweight expense record, not just a quantity log.
Stock-out entries require purpose and structural-location tagging (e.g., wall, footing, beam, shuttering, above/below ground level) to support later billing reconciliation.
9.4 Billing Reconciliation & Wastage/Misuse Flagging
Admin/Supervisor can enter the client-billed or engineering-abstract quantity for a structural element/job.
System compares issued quantity vs. billed quantity and computes the variance.
Variance within a configurable wastage tolerance is treated as normal; variance beyond tolerance is flagged for management review as possible misuse or loss — without assigning blame automatically.
9.5 Inter-Site Stock & Tool Transfer
Request/confirm transfer workflow supporting any site as both source and destination (not limited to office-to-site), for both materials and tools, with full transfer history including the transporting vehicle.
9.6 Tools & Equipment Management
Tool master with current location, ownership/rental terms, usage-hours/trip logging feeding into job costing, and damage/loss logging.
Condition status (working / damaged / under repair) recorded whenever a tool is returned to the office store, in addition to its returnable/non-returnable flag.
9.7 Labour Attendance & Multi-Site Cost Allocation
Worker/contractor master supporting a crew-lead ("Sarder") layer between the contractor and individual workers, where applicable.
Daily attendance marked by Supervisor or Contractor (subject to confirmation), recorded as headcount split by trade — Mistry (skilled) vs Labour (general) — with support for half-day/fractional attendance.
Attendance entries support a remarks/allocation field specifying how many of a contractor's workers worked at each site/location that day, and an optional task tag (e.g., electrical work, masonry).
Advances can be recorded inline against a specific day's attendance entry, in addition to standalone advance payments.
Wage calculation nets attendance against agreed rate and prior advances; the resulting payment is apportioned across sites according to the recorded allocation, rather than posted in full to a single site.
9.8 Labour Camp & Personal Products
Camp supply stock (food, gas, bedding) tracked with running balance, optionally linked to site cost.
Personal product/PPE distribution logged by item, quantity, and recipient (worker or bulk site-level).
9.9 Daily Site Capital & Expense Ledger
Office can log a capital transfer to a site, recorded as "capital in" at that site.
Site expenses are logged separately, categorized (grocery, hardware, labour, travel, misc./consumables/other — including one-off vendor lump-sum payments that don't fit a material category), against the site's running capital balance.
System design explicitly prevents a capital-in entry from also being counted as an expense entry, addressing the double-counting risk described by the manager.
Running capital balance visible to both the Supervisor (their own site) and Company Admin (all sites).
9.10 Job/Site Costing & Monthly Roll-Up
Aggregate material + labour + camp + travel + hardware + misc. cost per site per month.
Compare monthly cost against the amount billed to the client for that site/period to show a basic profitability view.
9.11 Vendor & Purchase Management
Vendor master; purchase entries recording vendor, material, quantity, rate, and amount, automatically updating stock at the receiving site.
9.12 Transport & Vehicle Fleet (Lightweight)
Transport log for material/labour movement between sites.
Vehicle master with fuel log entries, daily odometer readings, and servicing due-date tracking.
Compliance calendar for vehicle tax/pollution certificate expiry and company-level renewal dates (GST valuation, Udyog/MSME registration), with configurable reminders.
9.13 Office Store & Asset Register
Store room inventory of office equipment/machinery; items issued out are logged as returnable or non-returnable, with return status tracked.
9.14 Reporting & Dashboards
Company Admin dashboard: consolidated view across all sites — stock, tool locations, labour cost, capital/expense summary, vendor balances, and site profitability.
Supervisor/Contractor dashboards scoped to their own site/crew.
Standard reports: site-wise stock statement, vendor ledger, wastage/misuse report, labour cost allocation report, daily capital/expense report, monthly site cost vs. billing report, vehicle/compliance status report — all exportable (Excel/PDF).

12. Non-Functional Requirements
Usability: Site Supervisor and Contractor interfaces must be simple enough for basic-smartphone-literate users — minimal fields, large touch targets.
Data sensitivity: vendor-ledger and site-capital/expense data should be visible only to Company Admin and the relevant site's Supervisor — not shared broadly — given the informal-ledger sensitivity described in the discovery call.
Reliability: data entry should tolerate intermittent site connectivity, with retry/queue on poor networks.
Security: strict role-based data isolation; encrypted credential storage; full audit trail.
Scalability: architecture should support adding sites, materials, vendors, or vehicles without structural rework — including the currently out-of-scope 7 additional sites and 3 production units, if added later.
Export: all key reports exportable to Excel/PDF.
13. Assumptions & Open Questions
Confirm whether Phase 1 covers only the original 8 sites, or should also include the remaining ~3 sites and the 3 production units mentioned in the call.
Confirm the wastage tolerance threshold per material category for billing reconciliation flags.
Confirm who should have visibility into vendor running balances and site capital/expense data beyond Company Admin — e.g., should a future "Accounts" role be introduced.
Confirm whether informal (non-invoiced) vendor entries should ever sync to Tally, or remain permanently separate as operational-only records.
Confirm site-level internet reliability, to finalize whether offline-tolerant entry is required in Phase 1.
Confirm whether vehicle/compliance tracking should include the 2 vehicles used for the owner's separate plywood business.
Confirm the organizational rollout plan — the manager has proposed a minimum of 2–3 office roles plus one in-charge per site; confirm target role structure so the system's permission model matches real staffing.
14. Organizational Recommendation
Based directly on the manager's own observation, a system alone will not fix the underlying single-person bottleneck. The following staffing split was suggested during the call and is recommended alongside the system rollout:
One office role covering accounts and machinery/tool tracking.
One office role covering site materials and billing reconciliation.
One accountable in-charge per site (in addition to existing Supervisors), responsible for daily stock, labour, and expense entry at that site.
A realistic training runway of several months per new staff member, combining system training with hands-on site visits — the manager noted that group-chat-only training has not worked in the past.
15. Future Scope (Phase 2+)
Engineering support tools: Bar Bending Schedule (BBS) calculation and structural abstract-quantity generation.
Integration of the 3 production units and personal-business vehicle tracking, if confirmed in scope.
Biometric/RFID attendance and offline-first mobile app.
Automated reorder-level alerts and predictive cost/budget tracking.
Optional formal accounting/Tally integration, subject to the company's accountant's guidance.
16. Success Metrics
100% of active sites recording stock, labour, and expense data in the new system, replacing WhatsApp-photo ledgers.
Vendor running balances and site capital accounts reconcile with zero double-counting incidents.
Billing reconciliation flags material variances within days of stock-out, rather than months later.
Labour cost allocation matches actual site deployment for contractor crews working across multiple sites.
Monthly site cost vs. billing report available on-demand, replacing manual compilation.
